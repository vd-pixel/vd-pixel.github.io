The Adolescent Transition

[span_4](start_span)[span_5](start_span)

The transition from late adolescence into young adulthood is characterized by profound neurobiological and cognitive transformations[span_4](end_span)[span_5](end_span). [span_6](start_span)During this phase, the brain undergoes significant structural reorganization, particularly in the prefrontal cortex—the center of logic and impulse control[span_6](end_span).

Managing RSD

[span_7](start_span)

Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) involves extreme emotional pain from perceived rejection[span_7](end_span). [span_8](start_span)For a teenager, the brain can misinterpret social ambiguity as a severe physical threat, triggering a fight-or-flight response[span_8](end_span).

Stoicism and Rumination

[span_9](start_span)

Rumination is a cycle of repetitive overthinking that can damage self-esteem[span_9](end_span). [span_10](start_span)Stoic philosophy, specifically the "Dichotomy of Control," helps combat this by teaching individuals to focus only on what is within their power: their own thoughts and actions[span_10](end_span).
